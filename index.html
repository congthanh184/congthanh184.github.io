Bio

I'm a Product Manager with an engineering soul. For a time, I thought moving into product leadership meant trading the IDE for PRDs—and I missed the "click" of building.

AI changed that. Today, I leverage an AI-augmented workflow to close the gap between strategy and execution, moving from user stories straight to functional code. I've found my sweet spot in the "0 to 1" phase, specifically within FinTech, QR Payments, and Web3.

But moving fast is only half the battle. I use AI to build faster, and I use my engineering roots to build better. By prioritizing rigorous testing, security, and CI/CD, I ensure that every project—from PRD to production—is as maintainable and stable as it is innovative.

NemoSwap

A decentralised exchange built on the RENEC blockchain, featuring cross-chain bridging, a fiat-crypto P2P market and gasless transactions for easy onboarding.

A standalone exchange that I was responsible for, from design to launch. The goal was to bring DeFi to non-tech users. We tackled this with a decentralised P2P market, gasless transactions, and a non-custodial wallet with email login. Lessons? The cost of decentralisation is high — especially when users don't prioritise it yet. Also, the dark theme hindered growth — users over 30, especially those with reduced contrast sensitivity or eye strain, struggled to read the interface clearly.

Mystic Tarot

A tarot reading app designed for quick, on-the-go readings — ask your question anytime during the day.

What better way to practise LLM techniques than building a Tarot Reader? This was my early experiment with LLMs — fully coded by Claude. It taught me both prompt engineering and (surprisingly) SSR fundamentals: managing API keys server-side and lazy-loading 70+ HD card illustrations to avoid performance bottlenecks. My approach? Build a minimal working example outside the main project first, then integrate.
I also evolved from single-prompt readings to a two-phase Think → Refine flow: one prompt interprets the cards and question; another combines the reading, translates it to the user's language, and summarises. The process required a tight feedback loop — build prompt, evaluate result, tune, repeat. Good fun.
